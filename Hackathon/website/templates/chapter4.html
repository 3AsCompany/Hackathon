Chapter 4 Practical Geometry

Question 1

To construct a rectangle, we need to know:

(a) All the interior angles

(b) All the Sides

(c) Only Length and breadth

(d) Only one angle measure


Answer: (c)

Question 2

If two diagonals are given, then we can construct a:

(a) Rhombus

(b) Rectangle

(c) Kite

(d) Parallelogram


Answer: (a)

Question 3

How many measurements are required to construct a quadrilateral, uniquely?

(a) Four

(b) Five

(c) Six

(d) Three


Answer: (b)

Question 4

To construct a parallelogram we need to know:

(a) Length of its parallel sides

(b) Measure of interior angles

(c) Two adjacent sides and one angle

(d) Two adjacent sides and two angles


Answer: (c)

Question 5

If two diagonals and three sides are given, then:

(a) A quadrilateral cannot be constructed

(b) A quadrilateral can be constructed

(c) Insufficient information

(d) Any polygon can be constructed


Answer: (b)

Question 6

To construct a quadrilateral, we need to know two adjacent side and _____ angles.

(a) One

(b) Two

(c) Three

(d) All four angles


Answer: (c)

Question 7

To construct a quadrilateral, we need to know two diagonals and _____ sides.

(a) One

(b) Two

(c) Three

(d) All four sides


Answer: (c)

Question 8

To construct a quadrilateral, we need to know three sides and _____ included angles.

(a) One

(b) Two

(c) Three

(d) All four angles


Answer: (b)

Question 9

To construct a square, we need to know:

(a) All the interior angles

(b) All the side lengths

(c) Only one interior angle

(d) Only one side length


Answer: (d)

Question 10

What is the appropriate condition to construct a quadrilateral?

(a) When four sides and one diagonal are given

(b) When three sides and one diagonal are given

(c) When two sides and one diagonal are given

(d) None of the above


Answer: (a)

Question 11

A simple closed curve made up of only ______________ is called a polygon.

(a) curves

(b) line segments

(c) lines

(d) closed curves


Answer: (b)

Question 12

A polygon with minimum number of sides is

(a) Pentagon

(b) Square

(c) triangle

(d) angle


Answer: (c)

Question 13

A parallelogram each of whose angles measures 90° is ______________.

(a) rectangle

(b) rhombus

(c) kite

(d) trapezium


Answer: (a)
Question 14

A parallelogram whose all sides are equal is called

(a) square

(b) rhombus

(c) rectangle

(d) trapezium


Answer: (b)

Question 15

the diagonals of a rhombus bisect each other at ______________ angles.

(a) acute

(b) right

(c) obtuse

(d) reflex


Answer: (b)

Question 16

Diagonals of a rectangle:

(a) equal to each other

(b) not equal

(c) one is double of the other

(d) none of these


Answer: (a)

Question 17

The diagonals of a square bisect each other at ______________ angle.

(a) acute

(b) right

(c) obtuse

(d) reflex


Answer: (b)

Question 18

Minimum possible interior angle in a regular polygon is ______________.

(a) 70°

(b) 60°

(c) 90°

(d) 120°


Answer: (b)

Question 19

Maximum possible exterior angle in a regular polygon is ______________.

(a) 70°

(b) 60°

(c) 90°

(d) 120°


Answer: (d)

Question 20

How many sides does a heptagon have ?

(a) 2

(b) 4

(c) 7

(d) 5


Answer: (c)
